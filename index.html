Analytics and Systems Intern

May-August 2015 | Fremont, CA

I spent my last summer at the main Tesla factory as a Systems and Analytics Intern for the Human Resources Department. The beginning of this opportunity entailed of quickly picking up and understanding the fundamentals of Workday (Tesla's central employee database) with the purpose of creating headcount data reports. Eventually, I created reports and dashboards (collections of reports) that consisted of headcounts representing crucial employee areas of interest including gender/region versus employee title and a dashboard for managers to view statistics and information on their teams. As I became more versed in Workday, I installed validation rules implanted in employee processes such that errors will be thrown if insufficient information is entered within certain fields (such as employee hourly wage being greater than minimum wage and excluding discontinued locations). In addition, I helped to develop a Python program that would extract key information from offer letter pdfs to ensure that such data corresponded and matched that of their Workday profile. Nearing the end of my internship, I helped transfer certain employee information to Workday through consulting a resume parsing company, utilizing their software to parse every employee's resume, and creating an integration system within Workday to successfully fill in every employee's education, job history, and language fields.

Projects

TL;DR

Worked with a team during Cal Hacks of October 2015 to create a web article summary program through a google chrome extension entitled TL;DR. This extension simplifies highlighted article and webpage text into choosable short/medium/long summaries that appear in a window below the extension location. My personal role consisted of coding much of the back end of this project, more specifically constructing the word parser and contributed to the algorithm's creation. The sentence data was split into various elements in one array and the algorithm chose to display the most important sentences for the summary output based on the summed ranked values of words in each sentence. Currently, this extension has approximately 300 active users.
